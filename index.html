Why Arch?

There are many reasons to chose arch linux. Many people choose to use arch to breath life into an old machine, as it comes with no heavy desktop environment on installation, and doesn't utilize any unnecessary applications. Therefore, Arch has very low system requirements. An additional and very much related reason would simply be the amount of customization that can be done. With arch, you can literally change everything about the os. You can change your desktop environment. You can change your bootlogo.You can even decide what kind of bootloader you'd like to use. This makes it ideal for those who simply want a customizable, flexible linux experience, something which distributions like ubuntu simply don't give to the fullest extent.

Package Manager

Arch linux has an advanced package manager. Called pacman, short for package manager, pacman is flexible, well optimized, and extremely versatile, with options to install packages from online repos, or from source like any modern package manager. In combination with the arch build system, which makes building packages easy, installing packages with arch is quite convenient, though configuring them may be a different story.

Customizability

Arch Linux is known for its customizability. You can customize nearly every aspect of the OS. You can choose your desktop environment, your bootlogo, and you can choose what type of kernel and bootloader you'd like to use. If you don't like linux, there's always ArchBSD, a FreeBSD implementation with Arch's features.
